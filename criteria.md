# Novel Plot Outline Evaluation Criteria

Binary (yes/no) criteria scored by LLM-as-judge. Each "yes" = 1 point.
The number of criteria is not fixed -- add or remove as needed for your domain.
Binary scoring is more reproducible and directly tells you which criteria failed.

## Criteria

1. **three_act_structure**: Does the outline have a clear three-act structure (setup, confrontation, resolution) with identifiable act breaks?

2. **character_arcs**: Does EACH major character have a distinct arc with an internal want, an external goal, and a transformation or change by the end?

3. **central_conflict**: Is there a clearly defined central conflict that drives the plot from inciting incident through climax?

4. **scene_specificity**: Does the outline include at least 10 SPECIFIC key scenes (not vague summaries) with concrete actions, dialogue hooks, or turning points?

5. **stakes_escalation**: Do the stakes escalate across the story? Are there at least 3 clear moments where the situation worsens or the cost of failure increases?

6. **thematic_depth**: Does the outline identify and weave a central theme (or thematic question) that is explored through plot events and character choices, not just stated?

7. **antagonist_motivation**: Does the antagonist (person, force, or internal) have a coherent, non-trivial motivation that creates genuine opposition to the protagonist?

8. **subplots_integrated**: Are there at least 2 subplots that connect to the main plot (thematically or causally), not just filler?

9. **genre_conventions**: Does the outline respect the given genre's conventions and reader expectations while offering at least one fresh or surprising element?

10. **emotional_beats**: Does the outline include specific emotional turning points -- moments of loss, revelation, betrayal, hope, or catharsis -- rather than just plot mechanics?
